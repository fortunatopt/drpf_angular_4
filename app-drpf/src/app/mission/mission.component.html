Our Purpose

Insurance covers most of the cost for the living donor for the actual testing, but the out-of-pocket cost for living donors’ and recipients’ procedures for capability testing can be mind blowing for both parties involved. Aside from the medical fees, like co-pays, often times traveling expenses can include flying, lodging, food, rental cars and more. We wish to assist the partnership of living donor and recipient in these financial aspects as well as assisting in the emotional roller-coaster that is the process of having a successful transplant surgery.

How we got started

It all started with a Facebook page. In August of 2014 the Beers Family started a Facebook paged titled, “Daddy Needs a Kidney” in hopes of finding a living donor for a father of twin boys. The father suffers from PKD, Polycystic Kidney Disease. In April of 2015 the page had found its way to the Fortunato family. The Beers knew Mr. Fortunato for many years and he brought the page to the attention of his new wife. On the spot it was decided she would see if she was a match and candidate to be a Living Donor. The Beers Family was living in Maryland at the time and the Fortunato Family is located in Southern California. Many tests had to be completed. Some were conducted in southern California but with the insurance billing it proved to be difficult to get all the testing done without traveling to the transplant center across the country. This trip would consist of days a testing, thus acquiring many expenses that are not covered by insurance. If testing came back and Mrs. Fortunato was not a match or for other reasons unable to donate a kidney, the Beers Family would have to do this process all over again with someone else. Through this the Fortunato Family decided to Fundraise for the Beers Family to cover costs associated with the transplant surgery. The relationship that developed between the donor family and recipient family sparked the idea of having a non-profit to support similar partnerships. To help both living donors and recipients in the financial struggle of transplant surgeries.

Why we got started

Many assistant programs are available for the recipient but few are out there for the donor. None look at the partnership of the donor and recipient and look to help both. This is the need that was acknowledged when our founder decided to start this company. We looked at not only the needs of the recipient, but also the donor and their families. There are many families out there looking and in need of a living donor to save a loved one, but mostly due to financial reasons living donors are becoming scarce. The need for advocacy for the importance of living donors and for financial assistance to these donors and their recipients is huge. We got started to help fill this void. To fill in where insurance stops and to promote the importance and need for living donors.
